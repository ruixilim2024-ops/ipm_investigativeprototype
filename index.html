A psycholinguistic profiling instrument examining MOE's bilingual policy through two lenses: language acquisition (academic ability, self-assessment, age of exposure, and frequency of use) and cultural proximity (cultural resonance and internal language hierarchy). The key outcome is a tension score derived from the gap between these two identity dimensions.

Academic Ability
LA — Academic
Algorithm — AA (primary weight in LA)
Grade points: A1=9, A2=8, B3=7, B4=6, C5=5, C6=4, D7=3, E8=2, F9=1 English weighted ×1.1 (inherent difficulty) HMT: 10% of HMT grade points added (HMT has same A1–F9 grading scale) Dmax = 8.9 (max possible scale difference) AA_gap = (1.1·G_EL − (G_MT + 0.1·G_HMT)) ÷ 8.9 Range: asymmetric — MT dominance → −0.99 EL dominance → +1.00
O-level grades are used over A-level: GP cannot isolate pure language ability, and only ~30% of a P1 cohort sits A-levels. HMT grade points receive a 10% weighting — rewarding genuine additional achievement without over-correcting the English–MT structural gap encoded in the MOE system.

03
Self-Assessment
LA — Self-Assessed
Algorithm — SA (secondary weight in LA)
4 modalities rated 1–5: Speaking, Writing, Listening, Comprehension S̄A_EL = (S + W + L + C)_EL / 20 S̄A_MT = (S + W + L + C)_MT / 20 Each ∈ [0.2, 1.0] SA_gap = (S̄A_EL − S̄A_MT) / 0.8 Range: [−1, +1] LA_gap = 0.7·AA_gap + 0.3·SA_gap Range: [−1, +1]
SA receives a 30% weight — lower than AA — because self-assessment systematically overestimates L1 and underestimates L2. Formal grades provide the objective anchor; SA captures modality-level nuance not visible in a single grade.

04
Age of Exposure
LA — mild weight
Algorithm — AoE (mild contextual factor in LA)
Age bracket → discrete score: 0–2 yrs → 4 (native embedding) 3–6 yrs → 3 (early / pre-school) 7–12 yrs → 2 (primary school) 13+ yrs → 1 (post-critical period) AoE_gap = (AoE_EL − AoE_MT) / 4 Range: [−1, +1] AoE enters LA as a mild contextual factor — it predicts likely depth of neural encoding but is not a direct measure of competence.
Earlier acquisition predicts deeper affective parity with L1 and lower metalinguistic distance, but does not override demonstrated proficiency. AoE is given mild weight in LA to capture the developmental context without overriding AA or SA.

05
Frequency of Use
LA — Behavioural
Algorithm — FoU
7 items rated on Likert 1–5: Formal/professional, Social, Family, Digital, Internal-Think, Internal-Emotion, Internal-Count Internal sub-index computed first: Internal = (Think + Emotion + Count) / 3 Then FoU average across 5 domains: FoU_EL = (Formal + Social + Family + Digital + Internal)_EL / 25 FoU_MT = same formula for MT Each score ∈ [0.2, 1.0] FoU_gap = (FoU_EL − FoU_MT) / 0.8 Range: [−1, +1]
Internal monologue is sub-indexed into thinking, emotional processing, and counting before entering FoU — these tap distinct cognitive systems (narrative self, affective access, automated retrieval). Counting is particularly diagnostic: it defaults to the earliest-acquired language and rarely shifts even in highly proficient L2 speakers.

Part 02 Cultural Proximity
Cultural Resonance (CR) and Internal Language Hierarchy (iLHI) are measured separately on bipolar ±2 scales, then combined into a Tension Score T = |iLHI − CR| / 4 ∈ [0, 1]. T is the primary outcome: higher T indicates a larger split between where you feel culturally rooted and which language you experience as prestigious, which is the operationalisation of MOE bilingual policy tension.
06
Cultural Resonance
CP — Dimension 1
Algorithm — CR
Bipolar scale: −2 to +2 per item −2 = strongly MT cultural resonance −1 = mildly MT cultural resonance 0 = neutral / no clear pull +1 = mildly English cultural resonance +2 = strongly English cultural resonance CR_raw = mean of all item scores Range: [−2, +2] Negative → MT culturally dominant Positive → English culturally dominant CR is kept separate from iLHI — they can and do run in opposite directions, and their absolute divergence is the tension measure.
CR taps the cultural identity layer of language — whether speaking a language connects you to family, heritage, and a sense of authentic selfhood. Each item has an explicit MT pole and EL pole to prevent unidirectional framing bias. A bipolar scale captures direction and intensity simultaneously.
Each item has two poles — an MT/heritage side and an English side. Select the circle closest to where you stand. Left = MT-resonant, Right = English-resonant. Centre = no clear pull.

07
Internal Language Hierarchy
CP — Dimension 2
Algorithm — iLHI
Bipolar scale: −2 to +2 per item −2 = strongly MT-privileging −1 = mildly MT-privileging 0 = neutral / no hierarchy +1 = mildly English-privileging +2 = strongly English-privileging iLHI_raw = mean of all item scores Range: [−2, +2] ── Tension Score ───────────────────────────── T = |iLHI_raw − CR_raw| / 4 Range: [0, +1] 0 → no tension (CR and iLHI are aligned) 0.5 → moderate tension 1.0 → maximum tension (poles fully opposed) High T: CR and iLHI point in opposite directions — the person feels culturally pulled one way but internalises prestige the other way. This is the diglossic split made psychological.
iLHI measures internalised prestige and shame — feelings that exist independently of fluency. Someone can be highly fluent in MT (high LA) while feeling ashamed to use it publicly (high positive iLHI). The absolute-gap T formula is chosen over multiplication because it locates where the respondent sits on each axis independently, making quadrant position and T interpretable together.
Each item has two poles. Select the circle that best reflects your position. Left = MT-privileging, Right = English-privileging. Centre = no clear hierarchy.

MOE Schooling Context
Moderating Variable
Role in model
MOE track is a moderating variable — it contextualises and is expected to predict T: eLHI (external) is fixed for Singapore: ≈ +0.7 English-dominant, encoded via: — English as medium of instruction — 1.1× AA weighting for English — Labour market premium for English Differentiated/exempt MTL track is expected to predict higher T — the institution itself de-emphasised MT, likely amplifying iLHI English-dominance while leaving cultural heritage (CR) relatively intact.
Participants who took differentiated or were exempted from MTL represent the institutional extreme — MOE itself signalled their MT was less academically serious. This is expected to correlate with higher T: higher iLHI English prestige internalisation combined with unchanged or residual MT cultural resonance.

function doPost(e) {
  var sheet = SpreadsheetApp.getActiveSpreadsheet().getActiveSheet();
  var data = JSON.parse(e.postData.contents);
  if (sheet.getLastRow() === 0) {
    sheet.appendRow(Object.keys(data));
